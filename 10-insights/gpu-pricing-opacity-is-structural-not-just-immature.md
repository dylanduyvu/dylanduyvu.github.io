# GPU pricing opacity is structural, not just immature

## Claim

GPU pricing opacity is not only a temporary artifact of market immaturity. It is structurally reinforced by Nvidia, OEMs, and VARs because bespoke pricing and unclear differentiation preserve margins.

## Why It Matters

This weakens any naive "build GPU Kelley Blue Book and the market gets transparent" thesis. A pricing layer may still be useful, but it has to work inside a market where powerful participants benefit from opacity and may resist transparency.

## Evidence

- 2026-06-16: Bernie said the market is opaque and may never become fully transparent because many parties want to keep it opaque.
- 2026-06-16: He said buyers do not actually know MSRP for new GPU equipment because pricing depends on buyer identity, size, and relationships.
- 2026-06-16: He described direct Nvidia access as rare except for the largest buyers; most buyers go through OEMs and often VARs, creating layers of middlemen that all want margin.
- 2026-06-16: He said OEMs benefit from unclear differentiation claims, such as premium claims around liquid cooling, because transparency would compress margins.
- 2026-06-16: He had not seen governments or sovereign AI buyers exerting transparency pressure yet.

## Implications

- A useful product may need private transaction access, underwriting workflows, or risk-transfer backing rather than public index publication alone.
- Public scrape data is likely insufficient for institutional pricing.
- Go-to-market should assume some market participants do not want more transparency.
- The product may be more viable as a lender/insurer tool than as a public market utility.

## Counterpoints / Uncertainty

- Sovereign AI, securitization, insurance, rating-agency work, or larger institutional capital flows could eventually force more transparency.
- Even partial transparency can be valuable if it reduces underwriting uncertainty for lenders and insurers.
- Bernie is commercially positioned around RVI, so his view may overweight opacity as a reason customers need protection.
