# Banks may prefer guarantees or swaps over insurance for GPU collateral risk

## Claim

For GPU collateral risk transfer, banks may prefer guarantees or swap-like structures over insurance because they distrust insurance claim payment and exclusions. The structure matters as much as the protected value.

## Why It Matters

This explains why Barkr's guarantee structure can be a stronger wedge than residual value insurance for some bank customers, even when both products stabilize collateral recovery. It also clarifies why "insurance" is not automatically the cleanest way to package GPU downside protection.

## Evidence

- 2026-06-18: Thomas said Barkr's guarantee is easier to set up and get going, though it depends on whether the client can recognize the risk transfer.
- 2026-06-18: He said some clients want insurance and some do not.
- 2026-06-18: He said banks often do not want insurance because they do not believe insurance actually pays claims.
- 2026-06-18: He contrasted insurance with swap or credit-default-swap-like structures, where the trigger can produce immediate payout and fewer claim-denial escape hatches.

## Implications

- The right GPU residual-risk product may need different legal wrappers for banks, private credit lenders, insurers, and asset managers.
- Discovery should ask not just "do you want downside protection?" but "what structure can you recognize, account for, and trust?"
- Insurance-market euphoria or bearishness may be less important than institutional trust in claim mechanics.

## Counterpoints / Uncertainty

- Guarantees may be easier to sell to some clients but could be weaker, less regulated, or harder to recognize for others.
- American Compute's residual value insurance may work better for customers that prefer regulated insurance products.
- The transcript does not capture exact legal terms or payout triggers for Barkr's guarantee.
- 2026-06-16 counterpoint: Bernie argued that insurance can have regulatory protection that warranties lack, and criticized Barkr's alleged market-crash exclusion. This makes wrapper choice segment-specific rather than universally pro-guarantee.
