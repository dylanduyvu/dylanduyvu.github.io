# Non-commodity compute framing supports neocloud valuation premiums

## Claim

The argument that compute is not a commodity is also a valuation argument for neoclouds: differentiated operator plus contracted cash flows supports longer GPU useful lives, pricing power, lower cost of capital, and higher multiples.

## Why It Matters

This ties market-structure claims directly to GPU residual value and neocloud financing. A merchant commodity curve implies faster depreciation, price-taker margins, and utility-style valuation. A differentiated contracted-flow story supports more benign depreciation and a software-like premium.

## Evidence

- 2026-06-11: Friedman argues CoreWeave's non-commodity framing is the keystone of a bundle around long GPU useful lives, operator moat, contracted cash flows, and reduced cost of capital.
- 2026-06-11: He says the depreciation debate and fungibility debate are the same fight in different clothes.
- 2026-06-11: He frames McBee's caution as rational and sophisticated rather than naive.

## Implications

- When neocloud operators argue compute is not commoditizing, interpret it partly as valuation defense.
- Residual-value assumptions, contract quality, and derivative-market design are linked.
- A credible compute commodity curve could pressure neocloud multiples and collateral marks.

## Counterpoints / Uncertainty

- CoreWeave may have real operational differentiation that deserves a premium.
- Contracted cash flows can matter even in commodity infrastructure businesses.
- The source is an interpretation of strategic framing, not direct proof of CoreWeave's internal intent.
