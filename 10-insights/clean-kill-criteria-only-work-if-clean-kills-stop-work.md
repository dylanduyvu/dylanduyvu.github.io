# Clean kill criteria only work if clean kills stop work

## Claim

Pre-committed kill criteria are only useful if a clean kill actually stops the project. If the founder keeps searching for more disconfirmation after a thesis fails on its own terms, the process becomes procrastination disguised as rigor.

## Why It Matters

Fast falsification is valuable because it prevents months of motivated reasoning. The discipline is not just designing kill criteria upfront; it is honoring them when the evidence arrives, especially when the answer is emotionally inconvenient.

## Evidence

- 2026-06-30: The GPU compute novation thesis had a pre-committed LBH0 kill-switch around whether exit demand exists.
- 2026-06-30: USD.AI and Nebius provided two independent, well-positioned signals that current-market seller-side exit demand is absent.
- 2026-06-30: The mechanism is coherent: a supply-constrained market leaves customers short capacity rather than long committed capacity.
- 2026-06-30: A third "nobody wants out" call would add little information and mainly defer the decision.

## Implications

- Once a thesis cleanly fails a kill criterion, mark it shelved or killed and move effort to a better problem.
- Preserve tripwires for market-conditional theses, but do not keep running the same invalidation test.
- Treat "more research" skeptically when it repeats an already-resolved kill test.

## Counterpoints / Uncertainty

- Some markets shift, so a killed thesis can become live again if the original conditions change.
- A kill criterion can be wrong or too narrow; the answer is to revise the validation system after the fact, not pretend the criterion did not fire.
