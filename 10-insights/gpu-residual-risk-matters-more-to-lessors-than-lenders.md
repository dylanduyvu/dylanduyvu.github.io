# GPU residual risk matters more to lessors than lenders

## Claim

GPU residual value matters differently by financing model: lenders mostly treat residual as worst-case recovery and focus on offtake, while lessors actually underwrite residual value as part of their return profile.

## Why It Matters

This changes ICP priority. If residual value affects lender downside but lessor upside, lessors may be a sharper customer for RVI, residual data, and recovery-floor products than generic GPU lenders.

## Evidence

- 2026-06-16: Bernie said lending and leasing are different models and lenders usually do not bank on residual value.
- 2026-06-16: He said lenders view residual value as a worst-case salvage scenario if the borrower defaults.
- 2026-06-16: He said lenders focus 99% on offtake.
- 2026-06-16: He said lessors can rely on selling equipment at lease end as a way to increase returns, citing 20-30% return potential.
- 2026-06-16: He personally prefers leasing risk because it tends to involve more sophisticated enterprise customers buying smaller quantities, even though leasing is a smaller market.

## Implications

- Discovery should segment lenders, lessors, banks, private credit, equipment finance, and crypto lenders rather than treating "GPU finance" as one buyer.
- RVI and residual-floor data may have clearer ROI in leasing than lending.
- For lenders, the value prop may need to be reduced LGD, higher advance rate, or credit approval rather than return enhancement.
- For lessors, the value prop can tie directly to terminal value assumptions and return profile.

## Counterpoints / Uncertainty

- Lenders can still care a lot about residual value when offtake or borrower credit is weak.
- If GPU collateral markets mature, lenders may underwrite residuals more directly.
- Leasing may be too small to support a large standalone market despite better fit.
