# American Compute Bernie SLA email reply

## Context

On 2026-07-01, Dylan emailed Bernie Margulies at American Compute asking whether operator SLA / uptime verification is a real lender diligence gap and whether it affects advance rate, RVI, lender comfort, or deal approval.

## Raw Reply

Bernie replied on 2026-07-01 at 12:01 PM ET:

> I don't think most lenders dig deep into the SLAs and ability of the operator to meet SLAs. They mainly ask if the operator has done a few deployments already in the past. I taked to a lender recently about SLAs, they normally just pass on those unknown/unproven operators.
>
> Maybe you could help lenders evaluate it? Because right now they skip evaluating it

## Extracted Signal

- Lenders often do not deeply underwrite SLA mechanics or an operator's ability to meet SLAs.
- The shortcut is track record: has the operator completed a few deployments before?
- Unknown or unproven operators may be skipped rather than evaluated.
- The wedge may be lender evaluation infrastructure for skipped operators, not only pricing improvement for already-bankable operators.
- This supports SLA/uptime verification as a real diligence gap, but suggests the immediate buyer value may be deal eligibility / approval rather than just better terms.
