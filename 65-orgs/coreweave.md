# CoreWeave

## Context

CoreWeave is a major neocloud / GPU infrastructure company. In the current vault, it matters less as a target account and more as a reference case for compute commoditization, GPU financing, residual value, and neocloud valuation framing.

## Relationship

No direct relationship captured in the vault yet.

## Useful Signals

- Brannin McBee argues compute is not yet fungible enough to trade like a commodity because goodput, MFU, and operational reality differ across providers.
- CoreWeave's DGX reference-spec framing may provide a compute grade, while goodput, MFU, SLA, and contractual terms become basis.
- Per Friedman, the non-commodity framing supports a valuation bundle around operator moat, contracted cash flows, GPU useful life, and cost of capital.
- CoreWeave's DDTL 4.0 / 5.0 spread and rating gap is a useful public-market print showing customer-contract credit matters inside GPU-backed debt.
- 2026-07-01: CoreWeave fell ~10-12% on the report that Meta is building a cloud business ("Meta Compute") to sell excess AI compute. Analyst read: neoclouds rely on hyperscalers like Meta for growth, and a customer turning into a competing supplier is a direct threat to the operator's growth and credit.
- 2026-06-30: Dylan Patel (SemiAnalysis) framed CoreWeave's *balance sheet*, not its chips, as the real risk - reportedly benchmarking above hyperscalers on performance, while the fragility is pre-selling compute before it is live and financing it with debt on contracts. "Watch the contract book, not the benchmarks."
