# Thinking Machines Lab

## Context

Thinking Machines Lab (TML), founded by ex-OpenAI CTO Mira Murati, builds Tinker, a post-training / fine-tuning API that abstracts away distributed-training and GPU complexity. Relevant to this vault as the clearest exemplar of the "value migrates up-stack from GPUs to the customization/tooling layer" thesis, and as a large, concentrated compute-demand node.

## Known Claims / Signals

- Raised a ~$2B seed at a $12B valuation (July 2025); entered talks to raise at roughly $50B (Nov 2025; some sources $55-60B). Raise status as of mid-2026 is unsettled.
- Tinker is a LoRA-based fine-tuning / post-training API; it lets developers customize open-weight models without managing GPU infrastructure.
- Reportedly does a few hundred million dollars of ARR - claimed (Dylan Patel, podcast, unverified) to be the highest known revenue of any of the ~75 "neolabs," despite no shipped frontier model.
- Early paying users include enterprises (Bridgewater used Tinker to fine-tune Qwen3-235B) and university researchers (Stanford, Princeton, Berkeley).
- Reportedly sources compute via Nvidia and Google Cloud partnerships.
- Went through notable talent departures and a stalled-round narrative in early 2026.
